# 6G·Cloud Research and Education Open Hub

**Subtitle:** THz-Band MIMO Beamforming and SRANiC-ISAC for Cloud Digital Twins — KAIST Contribution (Co-Research Institution)

## Funding Agency & Period
Institute for Information & Communications Technology Planning & Evaluation (IITP) · Lead: Yonsei University · Jul 2024 ~ Dec 2028

## Motivation
6G mobile communications are evolving toward **SRANiC (Sensing RAN-in-the-Cloud)**, where communication, sensing, and computing converge and RAN functions are virtualized and operated as digital twins in the cloud. For a cloud-side digital twin to faithfully reflect the real radio environment, physical-layer technologies that accurately model and optimize the propagation and beamforming characteristics of massive MIMO and the THz band are essential. Moreover, in a SRANiC-ISAC environment that integrates sensing and communication, communication and sensing beamforming must be designed jointly, and learning-based operation that is robust to distributed, unstable cloud-RAN conditions is required. Our laboratory (KAIST) is responsible for THz MIMO beamforming for digital-twin construction and SRANiC-ISAC communication/sensing beamforming, and—through international collaboration with NYU, KCL, and others—studies ray-tracing-based channel-model refinement, digital-twin generation, and federated-reinforcement-learning-based cloud-RAN technologies.

## Core Objectives
**Overall final goal —** Secure convergent communication-cloud technologies for challenging 6G SRANiC research and cultivate internationally capable "Naturally Globalized 6G·Cloud Leaders"

**KAIST (laboratory) objectives**
- THz-band MIMO beamforming algorithms and optimization for cloud digital-twin construction
- Joint communication/sensing beamforming for the SRANiC-ISAC system (international collaboration with NYU)
- Ray-tracing-based channel-model refinement and digital-twin generation, and federated-reinforcement-learning-based robust cloud-RAN technology (international collaboration with NYU and KCL)
